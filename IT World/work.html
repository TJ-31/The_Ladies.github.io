The IT Professonal

Joe Modica is a an IT Sales professional who has worked at IBM for 20 years. He has had more than 28 years experience in strategic roles across global IT services and extensive experience in the field of digital transformation. Joe is very passionate about helping organisations to leverage the power of digital innovations such as cloud, cognitive and mobile. He has tertiary qualifications in computer science as well as postgraduate executive qualifications from Boston University, and is an IBM Certified Sales Professional who can provide the expert advice you need to solve your IT strategy challenges.

What kind of work is done?

The IT Sales professional is a trusted client advisor who brings companies the solutions they need and helps transform companies with innovative industry and technical solutions. The IT professional generates new leads and supports in the closure of Infrastructure deals. The IT professional is tech savy and knowledgeable around infrastructure and lead sales engagements. They are clear communicators and a natural relationship builder, able to present and communicate technical information to close deals with stakeholders.

Some of the specific tasks include the following:

  • Lead multi-disciplined teams and integrating products and services required to meet clients needs and have assigned contract signings, expense, and customer satisfaction targets for the industry supported.
  • Mastering and applying expert knowledge of the client’s business, strategies and goals within the industry. Ability to form in-depth knowledge of the client’s major IT architectures, systems, information flows, applications and their links to customers and suppliers. Understand the framework within which an industry operates and the underlying competitive strategies.
  • Lead engagement teams responsible for the development of deliverables, such as client proposal and presentations. Drive and manage an effective partnership with the clients and conduct client negotiations at senior executive level reaching lasting agreements and commitments.
  • Develop relationships with clients and external associations to enhance position in the market to win new business.
  • Direct resource teams to ensure appropriate quantity and quality of technical/services resources are deployed. Lead team of professionals and provide ongoing guidance in the development of solutions.
  • Accountable for business unit and specific personal objectives, including contract signings, profit, expense, and customer satisfaction.

    • What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with?

    The kinds of people the IT Sales Professional interacts with are customers from the different Industries (Government, Finance, Retail, Telecommunications, Travel & Transport, Energy & Utilities). These people consist of: CFO, CIO, CEO, Application Managers, Infrastructure Managers, Networking Managers, Cloud professionals , Project Managers, Security professionals.

    Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

    The IT professional spends most of their time selling to new clients. This is mainly face to face client meetings or video conferences to identify new sales opportunities, discuss proposals, negotiate contracts, provide presentations and bring innovation to clients.

    In addition, there is a requirement to handle multiple tasks concurrently and easily adapt to new assignments, system, tools; produce clear, concise, accurate written communication, clearly conveying thoughts and ideas to peers and management and establish and maintain positive working relationships with peers and the management team

    What aspect of their position is most challenging?

    Some of the challenging aspects are:

  • Winning new business
  • Growing the business
  • Working on Tender Responses
  • Differentiating from competitors to enhance winning chances
  • Developing unique Value Proposition
  • Contract Negotiations
  • Determining the ‘Win Price’ to meet client’s budget
  • Accountable for profit, expense, and customer satisfaction.
